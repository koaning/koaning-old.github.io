Simple Simplex

I used to love teaching this introduction to OR class and I always found that students need visual input to explain how the simplex algorithm solves linear programming problems. In this document I will eplain the basics of both linear programming as well as the simplex algorithm interactively.

The problem

Imagine that you own a bakery that specializes in baking apple pies, big and small. You want to figure out how many big pies and how many small pies you should make every week in order to make the biggest possible profit.
The following things are known:

  • The two main limiting factors in producing pies are apples and work.
  • A small apple pie only needs 1 kg of apples and 2 hours of work.
  • A large apple pie only needs 3 kg of apples and 1 hours of work.
  • Apples cost 1 dollar per kg.
  • A small apple pie brings in $5 and a large apple pie brings in $12.
  • The supply of apples is limited to 40kg per week.
  • You work alone in the bakery and as such you only have 40 hours of work per week that you can allocate towards making pies.
Assuming that you will sell all pies, how many of each pie should you make if you want to have the most profit?
